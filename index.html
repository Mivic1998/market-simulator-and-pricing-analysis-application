Demand Parameters

Modify these variables to control how demand responds to price and income.

Vertical intercept — approximate maximum willingness to pay.

Slope / price sensitivity — higher values make demand more responsive to price.

Average consumer income used in income-based demand.

Share parameter controlling how strongly income affects demand.

Scale parameter for nonlinear demand; shifts the curve horizontally.

Controls curvature; higher values increase sensitivity in nonlinear demand.

Supply Parameters

Adjust these variables to see how outcomes respond to changing market conditions on the supply side.

Supply intercept — baseline price component for the supply curve.

Supply slope — how much quantity supplied responds to price.

Tax Controls

Apply a tax to see how it affects prices, quantities, and efficiency.

Per-unit tax applied to the market; raises consumer price and/or reduces producer price.
